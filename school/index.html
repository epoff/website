  • Woman gets kicked out of Corinth for her reputation

    Comments (15)

    October 28, 2022 | Posted by Marisa Poff & Shae Chirunbolo-McKee | Filed under books, death, fear

    Woman is forced to take her children with her as she gets exciled from her hometown, her husband's hometown and now Corinth.


    Ad


    Medea loves to plan revenge on others in a deathly way. She is known for killing people to get what she wants or to get out of trouble. She got kicked out of her hometown for betraying her father and killing her brother. She did this because Medea wanted to help her husband Jason collect the golden fleece so he would become king. When they get back to his hometown, Iolcus, Jason does not become king. Medea tricks the king's daughter into killing the king. The god's find out about what Medea did and they kick her and Jason out of Iolcus. They go to Corinth, and have 2 sons. When Medea finds out Jason is going to marry the princess of Corinth, she gets upset with him and they get into an argument. The king, Creon, is now scared of Medea and wants her out of his town immediately.


    Ad

    This morning a witness sall Creon at Medea's house and overheard Creon say, “Medea, sour-faced, glowering with rage against your husband: hear this. I order you now to leave this land and go into exile, with immediate effect” (Line 18). We can infer that Creon wants her and her kids gone and into exile immediately to protect his daughter and the city from Medea's evil and cruel intentions.


    Creon is scared and fearful of Medea.


    When we got a hold of Creon we asked him, If he was more worried about his daughter or city getting harmed because of Medea? Creon responded, “After my children my country is my greatest love” (Line 21). Creon loves his country and his daughter. He wants to protect them at all costs.


    Ad

    Witnesses say that the two were heard fighting all morning. Medea was also seen outside of her house begging Creon on her hands and knees to let her stay for the sake of her and her kids but Creon was not budging. The fear of her staying would allow her to harm his daughter and his country.

    When Medea realizes that Creon will not stand down to banishing her she asks him one last request. Medea asks, “One day. That's all I ask. Let me stay for one more day” (Line 22). She wants to stay so she can find a place to go to. Creon understands and lets her stay one more day. He thinks nothing will happen to his city.


    Medea is pleading with Creon for kicking her out immediately.


    As Creon leaves Medea's house, a witness overheard Medea shamelessly say, “There are still dangers for this bride and groom, and for the happy father” (lines 23). Creon does not know what she means by this but gets nervous because of what she has done to people in the past.
